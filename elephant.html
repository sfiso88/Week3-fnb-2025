ELEPHANTS

Elephants are large mammals of the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea. Traditionally recognizes, the African elephant (Loxodontia africana) and the Asian elephant (Elephas maxumus) are scattred throughout the sub-Saharan Sfrica, South Asia, and Southern Asia. Male African elephants are the true extant terristrial animals and can reach a height of 4 metres (13ft) and weigh 7000 kg (15000 lb)

All elephants have sereval distinctive features the most notable of which is a long trunk proboscis, which is used for many purposes. partipularly breathing, lifting water and grasping objects. Their pillar-like legs can carry their great weight. African elephants have concave backs while Asian elephants have smaller ears and convex or level backs.

Elephants are herbivorous and be found in different habitats including savanna forests, desserts and maeshes. They prefer to stay near water.
